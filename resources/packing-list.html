Camping Packing List

Proper preparation is the key to a successful campout. Use this list to ensure you have everything you need for a comfortable and safe adventure.

backpack Essential Gear

  • check_box_outline_blank Backpack or Duffel Bag
  • check_box_outline_blank Sleeping Bag (rated for weather)
  • check_box_outline_blank Sleeping Pad
  • check_box_outline_blank Pillow (compact)
  • check_box_outline_blank Mess Kit (Plate, bowl, cup, spork)

checkroom Clothing

  • check_box_outline_blank Class A Uniform
  • check_box_outline_blank Class B T-shirt
  • check_box_outline_blank Hiking Boots & Camp Shoes
  • check_box_outline_blank Moisture-wicking Socks (extra pairs!)
  • check_box_outline_blank Warm Layers (Fleece, Jacket)

thumb_up DO

  • check_circle Pack your own bag: You need to know where your gear is when it's dark and cold.
  • check_circle Check the weather: Adjust your layers based on the latest forecast.
  • check_circle Label your gear: Many scouts have similar items. Use a permanent marker.

thumb_down DON'T

  • cancel No "Smellables" in Tents: Never keep food, candy, or scented toiletries in your tent (attracts animals).
  • cancel No Electronics: Focus on the outdoors and your fellow scouts. No phones or gaming devices.
  • cancel No Sheath Knives: Only folding pocketknives are allowed (with Totin' Chip).
